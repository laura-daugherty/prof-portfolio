Journey to Lambda

After High School I decided to go to Culinary School. I loved cooking, love cooking, and will always love cooking. I’ve always had a scientific approach to the culinary arts that didn’t always jive with my more free-wheeling classmates. After school I moved on to working as a line cook in various restaurants, ending up at a brewery in Athens, Ohio.

In typical pre-software-engineer fashion, I proceeded to reorganize and standardize the recipe book. In addition to standardizing recipes I began a fermentation program for making pickles and sauerkraut for the restaurant.

After my time in the culinary world, I decided it was time to move on from the restaurant lifestyle and pursue a career working with a small jewelry designer. After working a couple different positions for her over the course of six months I was promoted to Showroom Manager. Here I really discovered my passion for doing MORE in an organization.

Working at Colleen Mauer Designs I had the opportunity to make direct changes to the business and see the effects my changes had on sales and how the business is run, but more importantly, I could see the effect I had on the customers.

One of the last projects I worked on, and one of my favorites was to write a manual for running the shop. Up until this point there was no source of truth for information about how to run the business. I created a physical and digital book encompassing everything from how to process returns, make coupons for the website, information about jewelry materials to how to access security footage.

All of these experiences have culminated in a well-rounded, experienced employee with an itch to do more. At each of these organizations and throughout my time working I've tried to go above and beyond for each organization, pushing my team and myself towards excellence. I hope to find a position that affords me the ability to use my skills to positively impact the customer and my team.
